Hobbies

READING

Unlike most of my friends and peers, I was a bookworm.
My friends would come over to our house to play with me, but they would end up playing with my toys whilst I read books.
My favourite was *Cinderella*.
Even now, I enjoy a good book.

BIKE RIDING

At the age of seven, my dad bought me a bike as a gift for excelling in my studies.
Riding it up and down the driveway was exciting, however, the most vivid memory I have is of me crashing into the pavement.
I even spun a full 360 in the air before landing, a moment that felt both terrifying and cinematic.
The scars from that experience remain, yet it sparked a lasting love for cycling.

Toys

DOCTOR SET

As a young girl, I was captivated by Doc McStuffins, her kindness, her confidence, and her magical ability to heal toys.
I was convinced I wanted to be just like her.
Seeing my excitement, my supportive parents bought me a doctor’s set, complete with a tiny stethoscope and bandages.
I spent hours diagnosing teddy bears and curing imaginary ailments, believing I was destined for medicine.
Now, years later, I’m an aspiring software developer, a far cry from toy check-ups and plush surgeries.
If only younger me knew that her dream would quietly evolve into something different, yet equally exciting.
That doctor set may have gathered dust, but the encouragement behind it still fuels me today.

TIGGET THE TIGER

I first saw Tigget sitting on a shelf in a toy shop, his orange and black fur glowing under the lights and his stitched smile practically calling my name.
I fell in love instantly.
But my mum, ever the practical one, didn’t buy him that day and I left the shop with a heavy heart.
Then one evening, as she walked in from work, she pulled Tigget out of her bag and my heart leaped.
I was overjoyed. I hugged him so tightly I thought I’d never let go.
To this day, Tigget still lives on my bed, a little worn but deeply loved.
